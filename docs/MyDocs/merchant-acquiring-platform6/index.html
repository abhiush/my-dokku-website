To launch a comprehensive Merchant Acquiring product, dealing with the end-to-end product lifecycle from merchant onboarding to transaction processing, clearing and settlement, ledger account management, and chargeback handling, you can leverage the following BIAN (Banking Industry Architecture Network) service domains and their capabilities:

Party Data Management

Capabilities:​

  • Manage merchant profiles, perform KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) checks.

Relevant BIAN Service Domain:​

  • Party Data Management.

Product Management

Capabilities:​

Develop and manage merchant acquiring products and services, including pricing, features, and terms.

Relevant BIAN Service Domain:​

  • Product Management.
  • Product Deployment

Capabilities: Deploy merchant acquiring products to the market, ensure availability, and manage product configurations. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Product Deployment. Customer Offer

Capabilities: Manage merchant offers and incentives, customize offers based on merchant needs and profiles. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Customer Offer. Sales and Service Planning

Capabilities: Plan and manage sales strategies for merchant acquisition, monitor sales performance. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Sales and Service Planning. Customer Agreement

Capabilities: Manage merchant contracts and agreements, including terms of service and compliance. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Customer Agreement. Servicing Event History

Capabilities: Track and manage historical servicing events, including transaction histories and service interactions. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Servicing Event History. Payment Execution

Capabilities: Process payment transactions, including authorization, capture, and routing. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Payment Execution. Payment Order

Capabilities: Manage payment orders, including validation, processing, and routing of payment instructions. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Payment Order. Clearing and Settlement

Capabilities: Handle clearing and settlement of transactions between merchants, acquirers, and issuing banks. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Clearing and Settlement. Financial Accounting

Capabilities: Manage ledger accounts, post transactions, and ensure accurate financial reporting. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Financial Accounting. Transaction Authorization

Capabilities: Authorize payment transactions, manage risk assessments, and ensure compliance. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Transaction Authorization. Chargeback

Capabilities: Manage chargeback processes, including dispute resolution and refunds. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Chargeback. Fraud Detection

Capabilities: Detect and prevent fraudulent activities, implement fraud monitoring systems. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Fraud Detection. Position Keeping

Capabilities: Maintain positions and balances for accounts, manage real-time updates. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Position Keeping. Customer Billing

Capabilities: Manage billing processes, generate invoices, and handle merchant billing inquiries. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Customer Billing. Customer Workbench

Capabilities: Provide a unified interface for managing merchant interactions and servicing. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Customer Workbench. Customer Support

Capabilities: Offer technical and customer support services to merchants. Relevant BIAN Service Domain: Customer Support. By leveraging these BIAN service domains and their capabilities, you can develop a comprehensive Merchant Acquiring product that addresses all aspects of the merchant lifecycle, from onboarding to transaction processing, and beyond.

To effectively manage a Merchant Acquiring product, you will need to address various business scenarios that cover the entire lifecycle of the product. Below is a list of applicable business scenarios that you should consider:

  1. Merchant Onboarding Scenario: New merchant application and onboarding Tasks: Collecting merchant information, performing KYC and AML checks, setting up merchant profiles, and configuring account settings.
  2. Product Offering Scenario: Merchant product offering and customization Tasks: Creating and managing merchant acquiring products, configuring pricing, terms, and features, and customizing offers based on merchant profiles.
  3. Transaction Processing Scenario: Payment transaction authorization and processing Tasks: Authorizing payment transactions, capturing transaction details, routing transactions to appropriate networks, and managing transaction statuses.
  4. Clearing and Settlement Scenario: Clearing and settlement of transactions Tasks: Handling clearing processes, reconciling transaction amounts, settling funds between acquiring banks and merchants, and managing financial adjustments.
  5. Ledger Account Management Scenario: Maintaining merchant ledger accounts Tasks: Posting transactions to ledger accounts, updating balances, managing account positions, and generating financial reports.
  6. Chargeback Handling Scenario: Managing chargebacks and disputes Tasks: Receiving and processing chargeback requests, investigating disputes, resolving issues, and coordinating with card networks and issuing banks.
  7. Fraud Detection and Prevention Scenario: Detecting and preventing fraudulent activities Tasks: Implementing fraud detection systems, monitoring transactions for suspicious activity, and taking preventive actions to mitigate fraud risks.
  8. Merchant Servicing Scenario: Ongoing merchant support and service Tasks: Providing customer support, handling inquiries and complaints, managing service requests, and maintaining service level agreements (SLAs).
  9. Compliance and Regulatory Reporting Scenario: Ensuring regulatory compliance and reporting Tasks: Conducting compliance checks, maintaining audit trails, generating regulatory reports, and submitting required information to regulatory bodies.
  10. Performance Monitoring and Reporting Scenario: Monitoring merchant performance and generating reports Tasks: Analyzing transaction data, generating performance reports, providing insights and recommendations to merchants, and managing KPIs (Key Performance Indicators).
  11. Merchant Offboarding Scenario: Merchant account closure and offboarding Tasks: Processing merchant account closure requests, ensuring settlement of all transactions, handling final reports, and updating records.
  12. Merchant Communication and Engagement Scenario: Engaging and communicating with merchants Tasks: Sending notifications and updates, managing marketing campaigns, conducting satisfaction surveys, and facilitating merchant feedback.
  13. Financial Reconciliation Scenario: Reconciling financial transactions Tasks: Matching incoming and outgoing transactions, resolving discrepancies, generating reconciliation reports, and ensuring financial accuracy.
  14. Risk Management Scenario: Identifying and managing risks Tasks: Conducting risk assessments, implementing risk mitigation strategies, monitoring risk indicators, and managing risk events.
  15. Technical Integration and API Management Scenario: Integrating with merchant systems and third-party services Tasks: Providing APIs for transaction processing, facilitating seamless integration with e-commerce platforms, and managing technical interfaces. By managing these business scenarios, you can ensure the smooth operation of your Merchant Acquiring product, covering all critical aspects from onboarding to transaction processing, settlement, and ongoing support. Each scenario addresses specific tasks and processes essential for maintaining an effective and compliant merchant acquiring service.
