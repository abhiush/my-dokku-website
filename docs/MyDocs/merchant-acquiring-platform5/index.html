Merchant Acquiring System: BIAN Service Domain Model

1. System Definition​

1.1 System Details​

1.1.1 System Description​

The Merchant Acquiring System is a specialized financial service System designed to facilitate the process through which businesses (merchants) can accept and process electronic payment transactions. This System integrates various services and technologies to ensure secure, efficient, and compliant payment processing. It serves as a crucial intermediary between merchants, card networks, and financial institutions, enabling seamless transaction processing from authorization to settlement.

The Merchant Acquiring Platform serves as the backbone of electronic payment processing for merchants, offering a robust infrastructure to handle transactions securely and efficiently. It encompasses a suite of software applications, databases, and middleware components, all integrated to form a cohesive ecosystem.

Built on the principles of the Banking Industry Architecture Network (BIAN), the platform adheres to standardized interfaces and protocols, ensuring interoperability and ease of integration with other banking systems and third-party services.

1.1.2 System Products​

The System operates on a multi-tenant architecture, allowing multiple merchant clients to use the same infrastructure while maintaining data isolation and security. Each tenant (merchant) has its own virtual instance within the System, ensuring personalized configuration, data privacy, and customization according to individual business needs. This approach maximizes resource utilization and provides scalability and flexibility.

In its architecture, the platform adopts a multi-tenant approach, allowing multiple merchants to utilize its services within a shared environment. Each merchant operates within its own isolated domain, ensuring data segregation and privacy while optimizing resource utilization and scalability.

1.2 System​

1.2.1 System Target​

The primary purpose of the Merchant Acquiring System is to offer merchants a reliable and efficient means to process various types of electronic payments, including credit and debit card transactions, mobile payments, and online payments. The System aims to streamline transaction management, enhance payment security, and provide comprehensive reporting and analytics to support merchant business operations.

At its core, the Merchant Acquiring Platform aims to facilitate the acceptance and processing of payment transactions on behalf of merchants. Whether through physical point-of-sale terminals, e-commerce websites, or mobile applications, the platform ensures seamless authorization, settlement, and reconciliation of transactions, thereby enabling merchants to focus on their core business activities.

1.2.2 System functionalities​

  • Transaction Processing: Supports the end-to-end lifecycle of payment transactions, including authorization, capture, clearing, and settlement.
  • Security and Compliance: Implements robust security measures such as encryption, tokenization, and compliance with standards like PCI DSS.
  • Merchant Onboarding: Facilitates the seamless onboarding of new merchants, including KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) checks.
  • Reporting and Analytics: Provides detailed transaction reports, performance analytics, and insights to help merchants optimize their payment operations.
  • Integration and APIs: Offers APIs for easy integration with merchant systems, e-commerce Systems, and third-party applications.
  • Customer Support: Delivers comprehensive support services, including technical assistance, dispute management, and chargeback handling.

Transaction Authorization The platform verifies the authenticity and validity of payment transactions in real-time, assessing factors such as cardholder authentication, available funds, and risk parameters. By interfacing with payment networks and issuer banks, it orchestrates the authorization process swiftly and securely.

Settlement Processing Following transaction authorization, the platform facilitates the transfer of funds from the cardholder's account to the merchant's account. Through automated settlement workflows and reconciliation mechanisms, it ensures timely and accurate fund disbursement, minimizing settlement risks and disputes.

Reconciliation To maintain financial integrity and compliance, the platform reconciles transaction records across various channels and systems. By comparing transaction data with financial statements, it identifies discrepancies, errors, or fraudulent activities, facilitating accurate reporting and audit trails.

Reporting and Analytics By leveraging data analytics and business intelligence tools, the platform generates actionable insights into transaction trends, customer behavior, and merchant performance. These insights empower stakeholders to make informed decisions, optimize revenue streams, and enhance the overall payment ecosystem.

1.2.3 System Data layer​

  • Merchant Profiles: Information about merchants, including business details, account settings, and preferences.
  • Transaction Records: Data related to each transaction processed through the System, including timestamps, amounts, and statuses.
  • Settlement Records: Details of the financial settlements between acquiring banks and merchants.
  • Compliance Records: Documentation related to compliance checks, audit trails, and regulatory adherence.
  • Analytics and Reports: Aggregated data and analytical reports generated for merchant performance assessment.

Merchant Data The platform stores comprehensive information about merchants, including their business profiles, contact details, and transaction histories. This data is utilized for onboarding, risk assessment, and relationship management purposes.

Transaction Data For each payment transaction processed through the platform, detailed records are maintained, encompassing transaction amounts, timestamps, authorization codes, and merchant identifiers. This transaction data serves as the foundation for settlement, reconciliation, and reporting activities.

Account Data The platform manages account information for both cardholders and merchants, facilitating fund transfers, fee calculations, and financial reporting. It ensures the integrity and confidentiality of account data through robust security measures and access controls.

1.2.4 System Limits​

  • Security Protocols: Implementation of multi-layer security protocols to protect sensitive data.
  • Compliance Monitoring: Continuous monitoring for compliance with industry regulations and standards.
  • Data Integrity: Mechanisms to ensure data accuracy, completeness, and consistency. Service Level Agreements (SLAs): Clearly defined SLAs to ensure high availability, reliability, and performance of the System.
  • Risk Management: Comprehensive risk management strategies to identify, assess, and mitigate potential risks.

1.2.5 System Transitions​

  • Merchant to System: Onboarding process where merchant data and credentials are transferred to the System.
  • System to Payment Networks: Transaction data is communicated to card networks and payment processors for authorization.
  • System to Financial Institutions: Settlement instructions and funds transfer details are sent to acquiring banks.
  • System to Merchants: Delivery of transaction reports, settlement summaries, and analytics to merchants.
  • System to Regulators: Submission of compliance reports and audit trails to regulatory bodies as required.

1.3 Business Domain​

The Merchant Acquiring System operates within the broader context of the payments ecosystem, interacting with various stakeholders including merchants, card networks (e.g., Visa, MasterCard), payment processors, issuing banks, and regulatory bodies. It addresses the growing need for secure, efficient, and flexible payment processing solutions in a digital economy. By leveraging advanced technologies and adhering to industry best practices, the System supports merchants in delivering seamless payment experiences to their customers, thereby fostering business growth and customer satisfaction.

In conclusion, the Merchant Acquiring System, as per the BIAN service domain model, is a comprehensive solution designed to meet the dynamic needs of modern payment processing. It integrates various capabilities to ensure secure transactions, regulatory compliance, and insightful analytics, providing merchants with the tools they need to thrive in the competitive market.
